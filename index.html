Amazon Lab126

At Amazon Lab 126, I worked in the Alexa Local Search Platform team. My project was to design and implement a service that would make calls to a NoSQL database to provide internal Alexa clients with requested data and perform query parsing to identify search keywords. I also performed unit tests and load tests to make sure the functionality and latency of the new system matched those of the existing system.

Research

ADEPT Lab

I am currently working as an undergraduate researcher at the ADEPT Lab in UC Berkeley. My projects involve semantic segmentation and computer vision. I've been working with the DeepLabv3+ model to train neural networks on the Cityscapes dataset, and my work involves replacing convolutional layers with "shift" modules which are require zero parameters and zero flops. The goal is to reduce computation in deep neural networks while maintaining state of the art accuracy.

RISE Lab

At RISE Lab, my project was motivated by the idea of being able to train neural networks on privacy sensitive information from multiple parties without having to reveal the data. I conducted benchmark tests on convolutional neural networks (CIFAR 10/100 datasets) and sequence to sequence models using alternative activation functions, which were essentially linear approximations of nonlinearities in neural networks like softmax.

Personal Projects

EthTracker

A personal website I developed to dynamically track the price of Ethereum. It features a real-time stream of price/volume Ethereum data, an interactive chart that plots closing price vs time, and an interactive timeline chart with a few notable events in the development of Ethereum.

EthWidget

An Android widget I developed using Android Studio to provide real time updates on the price of Ethereum as well as the 24 hour percentage change. With a minimalistic design, it updates periodically at 1 hour intervals and can be manually updated with the refresh button.
